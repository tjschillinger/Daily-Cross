Pete Lackey Says:

Why? – Two reasons:

Simply because acquaintance with God’s Word without application of it is not only useless but also dangerous. But more, I sincerely want my believing and behaving to conform to the perspectives of the Bible—a book written long ago and far away. And achieving this to any degree depends upon careful reflection on that Book with a strategy designed to help reach that goal. With that in mind, I’ve devised a ‘meditation system’ and ‘meditation calendar’ of my own that allows me to pursue these aims.
Step 7: Meditate on God

Finish your time by thinking through the implications of God’s Word for both your beliefs and behaviors. Every 7th day, you'll meditate on one of God's names or attributes. The other 6 days will be spent reading or considering God's creation. Whether reading or considering God's creation, do so with the daily question in mind.

  • What, if any, Problematic thing does this call me to believe about the Gospel that I may need to Defend?

  • What, if any, Concrete way does this call me to behave in my Worship of God as a limited person?

  • What, if any, Caring way does this call me to behave in my Walk before God as an imperfect person?

  • What, if any, Creative way does this call me to behave in my Witness to God as a Forgiven person?
