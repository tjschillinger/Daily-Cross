Pete Lackey Says:

Why?

Simply because I want the ‘mind of Pete’ to conform as perfectly as possible to the ‘mind of Christ’ as revealed in the Word of God. And an important step toward this, I think, is getting the words of the Bible into my head. But there’s more. For, as I’ve mentioned a time or two now, I view my role in the world—at least when thinking about devotions—as one of a priestly nature. And priests, among other things, offer prayers. But the Bible clearly informs us that sin cherished in the heart prevents prayers from being heard in heaven (cf. Ps. 66:18), even as God’s Word hidden in the heart helps prevent sin in the hands (cf. Ps. 119:11). Putting all of this together, I see the memorization of Scripture as an important component in fulfilling my priestly duties in the world. I’ve discovered over the years that the more Scripture I’ve stored in my memory the more enriching my meditation becomes. So I take this ‘act’ quite seriously.
Act 7: Phylactery Prep

Daily Bible Memorization Exercises: To help your mind conform more perfectly to the mind of Christ, devote a little time to memorizing the Bible. The more scripture you store in your memory the more enriching your meditation will become.
