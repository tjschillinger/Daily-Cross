Why this step?

To allow your mind to conform as perfectly as possible to the ‘mind of Christ’ as revealed in the Word of God. And an important step toward this is getting the words of the Bible into your head. But there’s more. View your role in the world—at least when thinking about devotions—as one of a priestly nature. And priests, among other things, offer prayers. But the Bible clearly informs us that sin cherished in the heart prevents prayers from being heard in heaven (cf. Ps. 66:18), even as God’s Word hidden in the heart helps prevent sin in the hands (cf. Ps. 119:11). Putting all of this together, see the memorization of Scripture as an important component in fulfilling your priestly duties in the world. Over the years, you'll discovered that the more Scripture you’ve stored in your memory the more enriching your meditation becomes. So I take this ‘act’ quite seriously.
Step 7: Memorize Daily Verse

To help your mind conform more perfectly to the mind of Christ, devote a little time to memorizing today's verse. The more scripture you store in your memory the more richer your meditation will become.
